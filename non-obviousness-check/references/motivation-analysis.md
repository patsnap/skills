# Motivation Analysis Guide

Use this file whenever the conclusion depends on combining D1 with D2 or D3.

## Questions to Answer

1. What exact claim difference does D2 or D3 supply?
2. Why would the skilled person look to that reference?
3. Would the combination likely work in the claimed context?
4. What evidence weakens the combination theory?
5. Is there a reasonable expectation of success?

## Common Motivation Sources

- explicit suggestion in a reference
- same technical problem
- compatible mechanism or interface
- known substitution
- regulatory, cost, or performance pressure
- evidenced common general knowledge

## Counter-Evidence

- teaching away
- technical incompatibility
- loss of the primary function
- no reasonable expectation of success
- different operating context or objective

## Minimal Matrix

| Path | Missing feature supplied by | Motivation source | Compatibility | Expectation of success | Counter-evidence | Strength |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| D1 + D2 | D2 | same problem + explicit hint | compatible | supported | none | high |
| D1 + D3 | D3 | market pressure only | mixed | uncertain | interface mismatch | low |

## Guardrails

- “same field” is rarely enough on its own
- “could combine” is not the final test
- common knowledge must be evidenced, not asserted
- compatibility and expectation of success must be stated separately
