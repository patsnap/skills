# EP Inventive Step Quick Guide

Use this guide for EPO-style inventive-step analysis.

## Problem-Solution Approach

1. Select the closest prior art.
2. Identify distinguishing features.
3. Formulate the objective technical problem.
4. Apply the could-would test.

## Practical Rules

- The problem statement must not contain the solution itself.
- Ask not only whether the combination was possible, but whether the skilled person would have made it.
- Treat bonus effects and post hoc advantages cautiously unless they flow from the actual differences.
- Be careful with partial problems and multiple independent feature groups.

## Common Traps

- picking a weak D1 that makes the invention look artificially remote
- writing the problem too broadly or with hindsight
- treating “could” as enough
- ignoring direct and unambiguous technical teaching

## Output Notes

State:

- closest prior art
- objective technical problem
- could-would analysis
- final EP inventive-step strength
