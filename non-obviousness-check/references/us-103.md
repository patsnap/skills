# US Obviousness Quick Guide

Use this guide when the conclusion must align with US obviousness practice.

## Core Frame

Anchor the analysis on:

1. Graham factors
2. KSR-style motivation rationales
3. evidence for and against the combination
4. secondary considerations when available

## Workflow

1. Identify the strongest D1.
2. List claim differences against D1.
3. Search D2/D3 for those differences.
4. Test whether a skilled person would have combined them with a reasonable expectation of success.
5. Add secondary-consideration evidence if it materially changes the picture.

## Strong US Rationales

- predictable variation
- known substitution
- obvious to try among finite options
- design need or market pressure
- teaching or suggestion in the art

## Common Traps

- assuming motivation from field similarity alone
- ignoring interface or compatibility problems
- using unsupported “common sense” assertions
- skipping secondary indicia when the record clearly contains them

## Output Notes

State:

- best D1 + D2/D3 path
- pro-combination evidence
- anti-combination evidence
- strength of the US obviousness case
