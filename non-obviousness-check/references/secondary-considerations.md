# Secondary Considerations Guide

Use this file only when real-world evidence may rebut a prima facie obviousness case.

## Main Categories

- commercial success
- long-felt but unsolved need
- failure of others
- unexpected results
- copying

## Nexus Checklist

Before giving weight to any category, ask:

1. Is the evidence tied to the claimed features rather than unclaimed extras?
2. Is the timeline consistent with causation?
3. Are non-technical explanations addressed?
4. Is the comparison supported by data, not slogans?

## Practical Use

- Strongest when the record includes comparative data or market evidence tightly linked to the claimed features.
- Weaker when the evidence mainly reflects branding, distribution, or unrelated product advantages.
- Use as a rebuttal layer, not as a substitute for weak technical analysis.
