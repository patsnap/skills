# Domain Playbooks

Use the relevant section before writing conclusions in domain-heavy runs.

## Semiconductor And Hardware

- Prioritize patent family breadth, node/process architecture, fab vs fabless,
  EDA/IP position, standards participation, and product shipment signals.
- Do not treat press releases or hiring as substitutes for technical position.
- Ask whether the company owns core process/control points or only packaging.

## Biopharma And Medtech

- Prioritize target/mechanism, pipeline stage, trial or regulatory signals,
  translational evidence, and freedom-to-develop constraints only as background,
  not as legal conclusions.
- Do not treat early papers as proof of deployable capability.
- Ask whether the company controls a platform, an asset, or only exploratory research.

## AI And Software

- Prioritize papers, benchmarks, open-source activity, product deployment,
  API/platform adoption, and evidence of real user or enterprise usage.
- Do not treat model announcements as technical leadership on their own.
- Ask whether capability is research-only, developer-facing, or production-proven.

## New Energy And Materials

- Prioritize material route evidence, manufacturing readiness, cost trajectory,
  scale-up signals, lifetime/safety data, and supply-chain dependencies.
- Do not stop at lab performance numbers.
- Ask whether the company has route control at material, cell, module, or system level.

## Industrial And General Manufacturing

- Prioritize process capability, standards/certification role, installed base,
  deployment evidence, and integration position in the value chain.
- Do not overread patent counts without production or field evidence.
- Ask whether the company owns a bottleneck capability or is only present around the edges.
