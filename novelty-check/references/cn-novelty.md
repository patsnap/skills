# CN Novelty Quick Guide

Use this guide for China novelty conclusions under Patent Law Article 22.

## Core Questions

1. Is there one prior disclosure that covers all claim limitations?
2. Does the risk come from ordinary prior art or a conflicting application?
3. Was the disclosure public before the filing date?
4. Is any non-prejudicial disclosure or statutory exception relevant?

## Practical Rules

- Keep the analysis in a single-reference frame.
- Distinguish ordinary prior art from earlier-filed later-published conflicting applications.
- Check whether the claim is defeated by exact disclosure, not by a mosaic of references.
- Record the disclosure date and source type for every key reference.

## Common Traps

- Forgetting to analyze conflicting applications
- Treating partial overlap as full disclosure
- Ignoring parameter limits or sequence constraints
- Treating unsupported inherency as fact

## Output Notes

State:

- strongest conflicting reference
- whether the risk is ordinary prior art, conflicting application, or both
- missing elements and uncertainty points
- final result with confidence level
