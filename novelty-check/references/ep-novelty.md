# EP Novelty Quick Guide

Use this guide for EPO-style novelty conclusions.

## Core Questions

1. Was the information made available to the public before filing?
2. Does one reference disclose every claim limitation and their required relationship?
3. Is the alleged distinguishing feature actually missing, or only described differently?
4. Is the user relying on a grace-period theory that is likely too weak for EP practice?

## Practical Rules

- EP novelty is strict and usually absolute.
- Public availability matters more than whether the public actually read the document.
- Keep the analysis tied to what the reference makes directly and unambiguously available.
- Be careful with selection inventions, sub-ranges, and parameter windows.

## Common Traps

- Assuming a broad disclosure always destroys a claimed selection
- Importing unspoken features from technical common sense
- Overstating grace-period relief
- Ignoring whether the claimed combination is directly and unambiguously derivable

## Output Notes

State:

- the strongest Article 54 reference
- whether the claimed subject matter is directly and unambiguously disclosed
- remaining gaps or selection arguments
- final EP novelty risk level
